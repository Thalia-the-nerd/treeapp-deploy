Little Havana Tree Corridor

SW 8th St, Miami, FL 33135

About This Location

Little Havana Tree Corridor is a vibrant urban corridor in Little Havana that's being transformed into a green oasis. This project aims to create a continuous canopy of trees along SW 8th St, also known as Calle Ocho, the heart of Miami's Cuban community. The corridor stretches for several blocks and will provide shade, reduce urban heat, and enhance the neighborhood's beauty.

Why This Location?

This location was selected for tree planting because:

  • Little Havana is an urban heat island with limited tree coverage
  • The corridor has wide sidewalks suitable for tree planting
  • The project will create a continuous green pathway through the neighborhood
  • Trees will provide shade for pedestrians and reduce street temperatures
  • The project has strong community support from local residents and businesses

Recommended Tree Species

Based on the urban environment and climate, we recommend planting the following tree species at this location:

  • Royal Palm (Roystonea regia) - Iconic Florida palm, provides excellent shade
  • Live Oak (Quercus virginiana) - Long-lived native tree with wide canopy
  • Gumbo Limbo (Bursera simaruba) - Fast-growing native with distinctive bark
  • Mahogany (Swietenia mahagoni) - Native hardwood with dense canopy
  • Black Olive (Bucida buceras) - Salt-tolerant with attractive foliage
