Miami Beach Park

4601 Collins Ave, Miami Beach, FL 33140

About This Location

Miami Beach Park is a beautiful coastal park with sandy soil perfect for native trees. This location offers stunning ocean views and is a popular spot for community gatherings. The park is managed by the City of Miami Beach and is open to the public year-round.

Why This Location?

This location was selected for tree planting because:

  • The sandy soil is well-draining and suitable for many native tree species
  • The park receives plenty of sunlight, essential for tree growth
  • The location is easily accessible to volunteers
  • Adding trees will enhance the park's beauty and provide shade for visitors
  • The trees will help reduce coastal erosion and provide habitat for wildlife

Recommended Tree Species

Based on the soil conditions and climate, we recommend planting the following tree species at this location:

  • Gumbo Limbo (Bursera simaruba) - Native, salt-tolerant, fast-growing
  • Sea Grape (Coccoloba uvifera) - Native, salt-tolerant, provides edible fruit
  • Mangrove (Rhizophora mangle) - Native, excellent for coastal protection
  • Pigeon Plum (Coccoloba diversifolia) - Native, drought-tolerant
  • Jamaica Caper (Capparis cynophallophora) - Native, salt-tolerant, attracts butterflies
